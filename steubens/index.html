The Backstory

Boston, 1945. Denver, 2006.

The original Steuben's was a supper club on Boylston Street in Boston, opened by Josh Wolkon's great-uncles in 1945. The Vienna Room. The Cave. The Blue Room. Big Band, jazz, swing. The Rat Pack ate there. Proms happened there. Sinatra happened there.

Six decades later, Josh took that name, that feeling, and dropped it into an old garage on East 17th Avenue in Uptown Denver. Regional American comfort food. A lobster roll from New England. A green chile cheeseburger from Albuquerque. Fried chicken from the South. The same everyday-diner feeling his great-uncles built seventy years ago.

"We just wanted an everyday neighborhood diner, serving all the things you actually crave. That's it. That's the whole concept." — Josh Wolkon, Owner
