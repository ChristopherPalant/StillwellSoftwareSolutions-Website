№ 02 Our Philosophy

A larder of Colorado, set inside the stone halls of Denver's historic Union Station.

When Mercantile opened its doors in 2014, Chef Alex Seidel carried with him the soil of Fruition Farms — the sheep dairy and vegetable operation he has kept in Larkspur since 2007. The menu is rewritten each morning from what the farm sends down that week.

Nothing travels far. Butter is churned in the kitchen. Ricotta is cultured in the back room. The lamb comes from animals we raised by hand.

№ 04 From Fruition Farms

Twelve acres. Two hundred sheep. One kitchen, forty-five minutes south.

Fruition Farms is chef Alex Seidel's sheep dairy and produce garden in Larkspur, Colorado — a working farm that supplies nearly everything on the Mercantile table. The sheep are milked by hand. The ricotta is made in small batches each morning. The herbs are pulled at dawn and on your plate by dinner.

What doesn't come from the farm comes from neighbors: Palisade peaches, Olathe sweet corn, Rio Grande trout, Yampa Valley lamb. The shorter the distance, the better the food.
